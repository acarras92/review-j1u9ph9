# Gap-Fill Research: Do Contractor-Screening Incumbents Already Serve Hotel Owners?

Date: 2026-07-18
Scope: Targeted research into whether Business Screen, COMPASS/1Form (Bespoke Metrics), TradeTapp (Autodesk/BuildingConnected), and Highwire already serve hotel owners/developers/REITs specifically, plus what hotel developers actually do today to vet general contractors (GCs). Research/analysis only; nothing published, signed up for, or paid for.

---

## 1. Business Screen (businessscreen.com)

**What it is:** A background-check / due-diligence investigation firm (Corporate Screening & Investigations, Inc., Rocky River, OH) offering licensed-investigator-verified reports, sold across many verticals.

**Construction & Real Estate use case page:** https://www.businessscreen.com/use-cases/construction-commercial-real-estate
- Explicitly targets "GCs and developers managing commercial real estate due diligence and contractor prequalification internally."
- Services offered against subcontractors/GCs: professional license verification, corporate record verification, OSHA violation search, insurance/COI verification, civil record (mechanic's lien) search, lien search, criminal record search, adverse media search.
- One (unnamed, unverifiable) testimonial: "a national developer found their previous screening provider was returning inaccurate results... Business Screen replaced that workflow with investigator-verified reports, custom scorecards built into their CRM." This is a generic real-estate/developer reference, not hotel-specific, and the developer is not named — **[unclear]** whether it involved hotel assets at all.
- No hotel- or hospitality-specific case study, testimonial, or marketing page found anywhere on the site. The "Use cases" footer list (Commercial Lending & Specialty Finance, Marketplace Platforms, Vendor & Supply Chain, M&A Due Diligence, Construction & Commercial Real Estate, Legal Services, Grant Recipient Screening) has no hospitality/hotel category.

**Pricing:** No public price list; positioned as quote-based / tiered by investigation depth:
- "Preliminary Due Diligence" — automated searches, minutes-to-2-business-days, cited as the low-cost/high-volume tier.
- "Advanced Due Diligence" — live investigator searches at the source jurisdiction, source documents attached, 10-year lookback.
- "Deep Dive Due Diligence" — multi-jurisdiction, associated entities/name variations, 20-year lookback.
- A separate cost-focused blog post (https://www.businessscreen.com/resources/how-much-does-a-due-diligence-background-check-cost) gives general market ranges (not Business Screen's own price sheet specifically, but presented as illustrative of the category): "$150 for a quick business verification" up to "$1,500 to $3,000 or more" for comprehensive/cross-border due diligence. Get Pricing is gated behind a contact form (https://www.businessscreen.com/get-pricing) — exact figures were not obtained (no signup was performed, per guardrails).

**Conclusion on Business Screen:** Sells into construction/CRE broadly, including implicitly to developers who could be building hotels, but has **no hotel/hospitality-specific product, case study, or marketing angle**. It's a horizontal due-diligence vendor, not a hospitality-focused GC-vetting product.

## 2. COMPASS / 1Form (Bespoke Metrics)

**What it is:** COMPASS (https://compass.bespokemetrics.com/) is a subcontractor prequalification platform built around the "1Form" — a single standardized prequalification form subcontractors fill out once and reuse across multiple GCs. Produces a "Q Score" risk rating. Owned/operated by Bespoke Metrics.

- Site: https://compass.bespokemetrics.com/ — product suite is SRP (prequalification), Eval (internal reviews), Pulse (supplemental analytics), Data Control (subcontractor data sharing), Connect (3rd-party integrations). No hotel or hospitality vertical mentioned anywhere on the marketing site.
- Partnership press releases confirm its customer base is GC-centric, not owner-centric:
  - ConstructConnect integration (Dec 2020): https://www.constructconnect.com/company/press-releases/constructconnect-enters-agreement-to-expand-preconstruction-platform-through-integration-with-compass-powered-by-bespoke-metrics — describes COMPASS as helping "general contractors" meet prequalification requirements; no hotel/owner mention.
  - Jones (insurance-compliance SaaS) partnership (Jul 2023): https://getjones.com/blog/jones-and-compass-by-bespoke-metrics-collaborate-to-enable-better-subcontractor-risk-management/ — again framed entirely around GCs and their subcontractors ("Jones has many top General Contractors and Real Estate Owners in the United States amongst their clients" — generic "Real Estate Owners," not hospitality-specific).
- Real-world GC customer example: Thornton Construction (Florida) uses "COMPASS 1Form" for its own subcontractor prequalification program (https://thornton-inc.com/prequalify) — a general commercial GC, not a hotel developer/owner.
- **ConstructConnect "Hospitality" vertical page** (https://www.constructconnect.com/solutions/hospitality) does exist and explicitly targets hotels — but on inspection it is NOT a contractor-vetting/prequalification product. It's a lead-generation tool that helps **hotels sell rooms to construction workers** working nearby ("Boost Hotel Occupancy With Construction Projects" — find nearby construction projects, contact workers who need lodging). This is unrelated to GC screening and is worth flagging because it could be mistaken for a hospitality-specific prequalification offering.

**Conclusion on COMPASS/1Form:** No evidence of a hotel-owner/developer/REIT customer, case study, or hospitality-specific product tier. Its ecosystem partner (ConstructConnect) does have a "Hospitality" page, but it sells hotels occupancy leads, not GC vetting — the opposite of what a hotel owner doing GC due diligence would need.

- Marketing describes "custom qualification," "risk analysis," and "integrated risk insights" for GCs/estimators evaluating subcontractors — general commercial-construction framing.
- No hotel, hospitality, or hotel-REIT case study, testimonial, or vertical page found in this search pass.

**Conclusion on TradeTapp:** No evidence found of hotel-owner/developer-specific marketing, case studies, or customers. Framed purely as a GC-side subcontractor-qualification tool (i.e., a GC uses it to vet its own subs — it is not sold to hotel owners to vet their GC).

**What it is:** A "Contractor Success" platform (originated at Harvard, spun out 2008; HQ Burlington, MA) for prequalification, safety/financial risk assessment, and inspections — sold to owners and GCs across capital-project construction.
- Prequalification solution launch (Oct 2024): https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/highwire-advances-construction-subcontractor-risk-management-with-a-new-prequalification-solution-302276989.html
  - Named customer quotes: Robin Van Fossen (Rosendin Electric — electrical contractor) and Benjamin Huelle (T5 Data Centers — data-center developer/operator). **Neither is a hotel company.**
- BOND case study: https://www.highwire.com/bond-case-study — BOND is a general building/construction firm (Boston-based); the case study is entirely about BOND vetting its own subcontractors across "hundreds of subcontractors," with no hotel/hospitality project references.
- Blog on prequalification process/best practices: https://www.highwire.com/blog/contractor-prequalification — generic capital-projects framing, cites a 2024 AGC (Associated General Contractors) study on subcontractor default risk; no hospitality-specific content.
- Webinar with Howard Shockey & Sons (a Mid-Atlantic commercial GC): https://www.highwire.com/webinars/shockey-prequalification-approach — again GC-side subcontractor prequalification, not hotel-owner-side GC vetting.

**Conclusion on Highwire:** Public case studies and named customers span data centers (T5), electrical subcontracting (Rosendin), and general commercial construction (BOND, Howard Shockey & Sons) — **no hotel owner, hotel developer, or hospitality REIT case study or named customer found**.

## 5. What Do Hotel Developers Actually Do Today to Vet GCs?

Two source types found:

**(a) A hotel-development-focused checklist site** — Hotel Development Guide, "Hotel Contractor Checklist": https://hoteldevelopmentguide.com/hotel-contractor-checklist/
This describes, in generic/how-to terms (not a first-party account from a named developer — treat as an industry-explainer, not a verified insider account), the prequalification criteria hotel developers "typically" evaluate before issuing a full tender:
- **Company experience**: hotel/hospitality construction track record, project size/complexity match, GC-vs-subcontractor role history, regional experience.
- **Local presence & organization**: HQ/regional office location, ability to open a local project office, familiarity with local permitting/regulatory processes.
- **Personnel**: CVs of the specific project director/site manager assigned (not just firm reputation), staff headcount.
- **Subcontractor network**: strength/reliability of the GC's own sub network, especially for hospitality-specific trades (kitchens, laundry systems, IT infrastructure, FF&E/fit-out).
- **Reputation & references**: reference letters, site visits to completed projects, references specifically from architects/consultants/hotel operators.
- **Quality & safety systems**: QA procedures, health & safety policy, local safety-code compliance.
- **Insurance**: professional indemnity and construction liability insurance, adequate limits.
- **Financial/corporate stability**: 3 years of financial statements, annual construction turnover/volume, current backlog/capacity, ability to provide **bank guarantees or performance bonds**.
- **Price/tender evaluation**: bid price vs. competitors, cost-breakdown completeness, schedule realism, treatment of provisional sums, payment terms, retention, contract/legal structure (form of contract, warranty/defects liability, dispute resolution).

This matches (and cites) RICS guidance on construction procurement strategy: https://www.rics.org/content/dam/ricsglobal/documents/standards/Developing-a-construction-procurement-strategy.pdf

**(b) Surety prequalification as an independent filter.** Multiple surety/insurance-industry sources describe how, on bonded projects, the **surety company's own underwriting** functions as third-party GC vetting before a performance/payment bond is issued:
- Old Republic Surety, "Surety Bond or Letter of Credit for Contractors": https://www.orsurety.com/blog/surety-bond-or-letter-of-credit-whats-the-difference — surety issues the bond only after evaluating the contractor's financial statements, credit history, and track record; "the surety company's bond protects the obligee from loss" if the contractor defaults.
- Merchants Bonding, "A Guide to Surety Bonds & Letters of Credit": https://www.merchantsbonding.com/talk-surety-to-me/a-guide-to-surety-bonds--letters-of-credit — frames bonding capacity growth as tied to "prequalification standards" and a "credit and experience" evaluation by the surety.
- Roughley Insurance, "Surety Bond vs Letter of Credit": https://roughleyinsurance.com/blog/surety-bond-vs-letter-credit — describes the underwriting "three Cs" (character, capacity, capital) explicitly as "an independent quality filter" for project owners: "A bonded contractor has been vetted by a surety, which has its own financial stake in the outcome." Also notes letters of credit provide no equivalent vetting ("only demonstrated access to bank credit," "tells the owner nothing about construction capability").

**(c) Government/public-sector prequalification questionnaires** (TxDOT, Michigan DOT, City of Tulsa — https://www.txdot.gov/business/road-bridge-maintenance/contract-letting/contractor-prequalification.html , https://www.michigan.gov/mdot/business/contractors/prequalification , https://www.cityoftulsa.org/media/18027/revised-new-contractors-prequalification-application-lines-5-11-2021.pdf) show the same underlying document set used across the industry for GC-side qualification: audited financial statements, bank/credit references, surety statements confirming bondability, OSHA injury summaries, equipment lists, and project history. These are public-agency processes, not hotel-specific, but they illustrate the standard document trail (audited financials, bank references, surety letters, OSHA records) that any rigorous GC-vetting workflow — hotel or otherwise — draws on.

**No source was found describing a named hotel developer's actual internal vetting workflow in a verified, first-person account** (e.g., an owner's rep or hotel REIT publicly detailing exactly which of D&B reports, surety letters, or reference calls they use, in what sequence). What exists is (i) generic checklist content aimed at hotel developers, and (ii) generic construction-industry prequalification/surety practices that would apply to hotel projects the same as any other commercial construction. **[unclear]** whether any large hotel ownership groups or REITs have publicly documented their GC-vetting SOP.

## Explicit Conclusion: Is There a Proven Substitute Already Serving Hotel Owners?

**No.** Across all four named incumbents (Business Screen, COMPASS/1Form, TradeTapp, Highwire), this research pass found:
- **No hotel-, hospitality-, or hotel-REIT-specific case study, testimonial, named customer, or product/marketing vertical** for GC/contractor vetting. Named customers found were Rosendin Electric (electrical sub), T5 Data Centers, BOND, Howard Shockey & Sons, and Thornton Construction — all general commercial construction or data-center players, none hospitality.
- The one hospitality-adjacent product that does exist (ConstructConnect's "Hospitality" vertical) is **not a GC-vetting tool at all** — it's a lead-gen tool selling hotel room-nights to traveling construction crews. This is a meaningful negative finding: the industry's closest "hospitality + construction platform" pairing has nothing to do with contractor due diligence, which suggests the vetting-tool vendors have not identified/targeted hotel owners as a distinct buyer segment.
- What hotel developers actually rely on today, per available secondary sources, is the **generic commercial-construction toolkit** — audited financials, bank/credit references, surety bonding and its underlying underwriting as a de facto third-party vetting filter, OSHA/safety records, and manual reference/site-visit checks — assembled ad hoc rather than through a dedicated hotel-owner-facing platform.

This is consistent with (does not contradict) a thesis that hotel owners/developers are an underserved niche for existing contractor-screening platforms: the incumbents are real, well-funded, and serve construction/CRE broadly, but none has built or marketed a hospitality-specific offering, and none has a public hotel-owner customer reference. **[unclear]** whether any incumbent serves hotel owners under NDA/without public case studies — absence of public marketing is evidence of a gap, not proof of zero private usage.
