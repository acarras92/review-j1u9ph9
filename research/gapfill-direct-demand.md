## 1. Startups with disclosed paying hotel customers (change-order-audit / PIP-benchmarking / construction-audit / capital-planning software)

**Bottom line: no company found in this sweep discloses named hotel-client paying customers, hotel-specific revenue, or a hotel-specific case study for a change-order-audit or construction-audit SaaS product.** This is a real, confirmed gap, not just a research miss — searches were run across general construction-tech ("change order audit startup," "construction audit consulting," "audit technology case study"), and every hit that came back was either (a) a general-construction/accounting product with no hotel case studies, (b) a professional-services firm (see §3) rather than a software vendor, or (c) unrelated (HOA software, AP recovery audit firms, academic papers).

The one relevant find, and the strongest single data point in this whole gap-fill, is **PIPGURU** (thepipguru.com) — a narrow, disclosed-pricing SaaS product for the adjacent "PIP-benchmarking" category:

- **Disclosed pricing** (from the live site, fetched 2026-07-18): Single Estimate $25 one-time; Starter $50/month (unlimited "Gut Check" estimates, 5 "Deep Dive" estimates/month, limited to 3 of 6 hotel tiers); Professional $100/month (unlimited Gut Checks and Deep Dives, all 6 tiers, branded PDF export); Enterprise $200/month (Professional plus up to 5 team seats, portfolio comparison, custom branding). Source: https://www.thepipguru.com/
- The site explicitly benchmarks itself against consultant pricing: "$5,000–$15,000" per estimate, "2–3 weeks" turnaround, replaced by their tool in "under 5 minutes." Source: https://www.thepipguru.com/
- One testimonial (not independently verified, not a named individual/property): *"What used to take two weeks and a $10,000 consultant fee now takes twenty minutes before our acquisition call. We've used it on every deal this quarter."* — attributed only to "Asset Manager · Regional Hotel Management Company." Source: https://www.thepipguru.com/
- **No named customers, no disclosed revenue/customer count, no case studies** appear on the page. [unclear] whether PIPGURU has meaningful paid traction or is a small/early product — this exact question is explicitly deferred to `gapfill-competitor-traction.md` per Gap 3 in critic-gaps.md; this file should not be read as resolving that question.
- Read as WTP evidence: PIPGURU's own pricing ($25–$200/mo) is priced as a small fraction of the $5,000–$15,000 consultant fee it displaces, i.e., the vendor is *itself* betting the WTP ceiling is consultant-fee-anchored but the SaaS substitute price point that converts is two-to-three orders of magnitude lower. That's a real market signal, but it comes from the vendor, not from a hotel owner quote, and carries the same limits as any vendor-authored pricing page (self-selected testimonial, no verification of the $10k figure it undercuts).

No other capital-planning software vendor found in this sweep (Actabl, Transcendent/Cayuga Hospitality, Quore CapEx, Hotel Investor Apps, CBRE's `pip.cbrehotels.com` benchmarking platform) discloses pricing, named hotel customers, or revenue on its public pages. CBRE's PIP/benchmarking platform (https://pip.cbrehotels.com/experience-the-platform) appears to be an internal CBRE tool bundled with brokerage/advisory relationships rather than a standalone paid product — pricing not disclosed. Hotel Investor Apps (ERP/back-office, not audit-specific) has raised $3.92M per PitchBook (https://pitchbook.com/profiles/company/482604-31), which is a funding signal for hospitality-adjacent capex software generally but not for change-order-audit or construction-audit software specifically, and not evidence of hotel-owner WTP.

## 3. What owners actually pay humans today

### PIP consultant fees — the $5,000–$15,000/estimate figure

**Verified via multiple independent sources, consistent range, treat as reasonably well-corroborated:**
- Multiple industry sources converge on "$5,000 to $15,000" for a preliminary/proprietary PIP cost estimate from a consultant, with 2–3 week turnaround. This figure recurs across PIPGURU's own competitive-positioning copy (https://www.thepipguru.com/) and general search-aggregated hotel-PIP-cost content (search conducted 2026-07-18, query: `"PIP" hotel consultant fee "$5,000" OR "$7,500" OR "$10,000" estimate report cost`).
- One specific sub-figure: "$10,000 to interpret a proprietary cost database for hotel PIP estimates" appears as a more granular claim within the same source cluster. [unclear] — this could not be traced to one single named, citable primary source (e.g., a named consulting firm's published rate card); it recurs in aggregator/blog content (e.g., DEV Builders, Sara Hospitality USA blog) that itself doesn't cite a primary source. Treat the $5,000–$15,000 range as the more defensible number; treat the discrete "$10,000" point figure as directionally consistent but not independently sourced beyond the same content cluster.
- Beyer Brown (a real, operating PIP/FF&E consulting firm with named hotel clients — Waldorf Astoria Bonnet Creek, OMNI San Diego, Montage Big Sky, Hotel Alpenrock Breckenridge Curio Collection — and named testimonials from Davidson Hospitality, LittKM Group, Hudson Advisors, Wheelock Street Capital) does **not** disclose pricing on its public PIP-analysis page; it directs prospects to "Get a Quote." Source: https://www.beyerbrown.com/services/pip-analysis-reporting/ (fetched 2026-07-18). This confirms PIP consulting is a real, actively-used paid service with recognizable institutional clients, but the firm itself keeps pricing private — consistent with (not proof of) the $5k–$15k range reported by third parties.
- Total PIP project costs (distinct from the *estimate* fee) run far higher: Holiday Inn Express "Formula Blue" ~$10,000–$25,000/room; Hampton Inn "Forever Young" ~$15,000–$40,000/room (aggregated search result, sources not individually verified — [unclear] primary attribution, treat as directional order-of-magnitude only).

### Owner's-rep fees (% of project cost)

**Well-corroborated range:** 1–5% of total construction cost is the standard range cited across multiple independent sources (Mastt, DAE Group, iRecruit, Watchdog Project Management). For large capital projects (>$5M), typical fees compress to 1–3% on a sliding scale. Hourly alternative: $75–$200+/hour. Sources:
- https://www.mastt.com/blogs/owners-representative-construction-fee
- https://daegroupllc.com/owner-representative-fees/
- https://www.irecruit.co/insights/owners-representative-costs-fee-structures-explained
- https://watchdogpm.com/blueprint/owners-rep-fees/ (Watchdog explicitly prefers lump-sum pricing over percentage-of-cost "so there are no surprise invoices"; the firm has completed 3,000+ projects worth $1B+ across 45 states, and lists a named case study — a 113-room Home2 Suites by Hilton in Ridley Park, PA, near Philadelphia International Airport, where Watchdog served as owner's rep managing the construction manager and consultants. Source: https://watchdogpm.com/project/test-project-5/)
- Applied to a mid-size hotel PIP/renovation project (e.g., a $2–5M scope), 1–5% implies $20,000–$250,000 in owner's-rep fees — several orders of magnitude above a SaaS subscription, confirming owner's-rep fees are not the right WTP anchor for a lightweight audit/benchmarking tool; they anchor a different (much higher-touch, higher-price) service tier.

### HOA reserve-study pricing (analog)

**Verified, tight consensus range:** $1,200–$6,000 for most HOAs, with tiers: small HOA/condo $800–$2,500; medium community $2,000–$5,000; large/complex (elevators, multiple buildings, private utilities) $5,000–$10,000+. Sources:
- https://www.reservestudygroup.com/post/how-much-does-a-reserve-study-cost-for-a-hoa
- https://www.propfusion.com/hoa-reserve-study/how-much-do-hoa-reserve-studies-cost
- https://nexovaai.io/blog/hoa-reserve-study-cost-california
- https://www.reserveadvisors.com/resources/blog/how-much-does-a-reserve-study-cost/
Update studies (no new site visit) are cheaper than first-time/full studies — a useful analog for a software tool that could price a "refresh" cheaper than an "initial" report. This $2,000–$5,000 mid-tier figure was requested in the task as an approximate check and is confirmed accurate for medium-complexity HOAs.

## What this means for the Gap 2 question specifically

The critic's concern — "no hotel owner has been observed saying 'I would pay $X for this'" — **remains unresolved by this research pass.** No first-person, price-specific owner statement was located anywhere (LinkedIn, AAHOA-adjacent press, forums). The strongest available substitute evidence is:
1. A real, actively-priced SaaS product (PIPGURU) that has picked $25–$200 as its price point against a $5,000–$15,000 consultant-fee anchor — this is the single clearest quasi-market signal of what a software substitute might command, but it's vendor pricing, not confirmed customer WTP (no disclosed revenue, no named customers).
2. Well-corroborated fees for the human alternatives owners currently pay (PIP consultants, owner's-reps, HOA-analog reserve studies), which bound the problem: a software tool priced anywhere near $50–$500/report undercuts the $5,000–$15,000 human-consultant price by 10–100x, which is directionally where PIPGURU already sits — suggesting the category's viable software price ceiling is much closer to "hundreds of dollars" than "thousands."

Judges should treat WTP scores for C1/C2 as still resting on proxy/analog evidence (consultant fee anchors + one vendor's live pricing page), not on any confirmed owner statement of willingness to pay.
