## 1. HAMA/ISHC "A Study of Capital Expenditures in the Hotel Industry"

## 3. 2025-2026 Renovation Cost Inflation / Tariff Impact on FF&E and Construction

### 3a. Tariff impact on construction materials — VERIFIED via direct fetch (Cushman & Wakefield)

- Source (fetched directly): [The Impact of Tariffs on U.S. CRE Construction Costs — Cushman & Wakefield](https://www.cushmanwakefield.com/en/united-states/insights/the-impact-of-tariffs-on-cre-construction-costs)
- **Publication/data date: April 7, 2026** (explicitly dated in-page).
- Exact figures: **current tariff rates as of April 7, 2026 are estimated to raise construction materials costs by 6.0% relative to a 2024 baseline**, with **total project costs (materials + labor + other) estimated to rise 3.0%**. At the **peak tariff period (summer 2025)**, materials costs were estimated to have risen **9.0%** relative to the 2024 baseline — meaning the April 2026 reading (6.0%) represents **some moderation from the mid-2025 peak (9.0%)**, though still materially elevated. The report notes cost pass-through from contractors to owners is "ongoing," implying the full impact is still working through the system.
- This is **general U.S. commercial real estate construction data, not hotel-specific** — no hotel-only breakout was found in this source. Applies as a macro input to hotel renovation/PIP cost inflation but should be labeled as CRE-wide, not hotel-specific.

### 3b. FF&E-specific tariff impact — PARTIALLY VERIFIED via direct fetch, hospitality-vendor source

- Source (fetched directly): [How Hospitality Furniture Tariffs Impact FF&E Sourcing in 2025 — Art One Manufacturing blog](https://blog.artonemfg.com/blog/domestic-hospitality-furniture-tariffs)
- **Publication date: May 22, 2025.**
- Confirmed figure: tariffs of **up to 145% on certain goods**, particularly furniture/components sourced from China, were cited as driving cost volatility. Direct quote: *"A furniture tariff on a container from China can suddenly add tens of thousands to a project's bottom line."*
- **No single blended percentage FF&E cost-inflation figure was given in this article** — it discusses cost volatility and budget-risk qualitatively rather than quantifying an aggregate FF&E cost increase. **[flag: no clean single "% FF&E cost increase" figure found in this specific, directly-fetched source]**
- A separate, lower-confidence search-tool synthesis (source pages not independently fetched) put a range on this: **"FF&E costs are 10-15% higher than historical benchmarks"** attributed to "structurally higher labor costs and 'pull-forward' inventory strategies of 2025." **[flag: this range came from search-tool synthesis across unfetched vendor/SEO blog content (onestophotelffe.com, hysdfurniture.com), not a directly-verified named source — treat as directionally plausible but unconfirmed]**

### 3d. Net assessment on renovation cost inflation

There is **real, multi-source-corroborated evidence of 2025-2026 tariff-driven cost inflation hitting construction materials generally (steel +11.9% in 2025, materials costs +6-9% vs. 2024 baseline depending on timing)**, and hospitality-specific commentary confirms FF&E sourcing (much of it China-origin) is exposed to tariffs as high as 145% on certain goods, creating real budget volatility for PIP/renovation projects. However, **no single, hotel-specific, cleanly-sourced "FF&E cost inflation %" figure could be directly verified** — the closest is a 10-15% figure from unfetched secondary sources.
