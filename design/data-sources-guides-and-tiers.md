## PART A — Publicly hosted copies of the Nehmer/HVS "Hotel Cost Estimating Guide" and JLL/CBRE capex publications

### A1. Finding: a genuine open (no-signup) copy of the Guide exists — 2021 edition

The **2021 Hotel Cost Estimating Guide** (13th edition, by Nehmer and HVS Design) is hosted directly on HVS's own web server with no login, paywall, or lead-gen form in front of it:

- **PDF (open, no form):** https://www.hvs.com/WebContent/bookstore/HVS%20Hotel%20Cost%20Estimating%20Guide%202021.pdf
  - Verified by direct download: 124-page PDF, 16.6MB, served with HTTP 200, no authentication wall.

An earlier edition is also openly hosted on Hospitality Net (no signup):
- **HOTEL COST ESTIMATING GUIDE 2011** (JN+A & HVS Design): https://www.hospitalitynet.org/file/152004649.pdf (older than the 2019–2026 window requested, listed here only for completeness; not used for figures below).

**Newer editions (2023, 2024, 2025) exist but are NOT openly hosted** — I checked:
- The current official distribution channel is **hotelcostestimatingguide.com**, which 301-redirects to https://www.nehmer.com/costguide/ . I fetched that page directly: it explicitly requires filling out a form ("To access the Hotel Cost Estimating Guide, please fill out the form below") before the PDF is served. Per your guardrails, I did not submit this form and could not obtain the 2023/2024/2025 editions.
- A 2024 edition appears on Scribd (https://www.scribd.com/document/797976706/Nehmer-HVSD-2024-Hotel-Cost-Estimating-Guide) but Scribd gates full-document viewing behind a login/paywall — also skipped per guardrails.
- Press coverage confirming the 2023/2024/2025 editions exist (no PDFs, description only): https://www.htrends.com/trends-detail-sid-126713.html (2023 release), https://www.hvs.com/news/10172/nehmer-and-hvs-design-release-2025-hotel-cost-estimating-guide (2025 release, confirms tiers/categories are unchanged in structure).

**Conclusion for Part A:** A public, form-free copy of the Guide does exist — the **2021 edition** — and it is granular enough to answer the question in full. All extracted figures below are from that verified document. Later editions are real but currently gated; I have not fabricated or estimated inflation-adjusted 2023–2025 numbers from the 2021 base — treat 2021 figures as dated (pre-2022/2023 construction-cost inflation cycle) and adjust upward when using them for current-year underwriting.

### A2. Guide structure and granularity (directly answers the question asked)

The Guide is divided into **six hotel tiers** (Economy, Extended Stay, Midscale, Upscale, Upper Upscale, Luxury), each assuming a prototype hotel size (e.g., Economy = 90 rooms/3 stories; Upper Upscale = 192 rooms + 25 suites/7 stories; Luxury = 200 rooms + 20 suites/6 stories). Table of contents (page numbers as printed in the 2021 PDF):
Economy p.12, Extended Stay p.20, Midscale p.32, Upscale p.46, Upper Upscale p.62, Luxury p.78, Industry Insights p.94, Sponsor Recognition p.100, Glossary p.106.

Within each tier the Guide gives, for every room/area type: **Softgoods Renovation** (per-key or per-SF line items with low/high/average unit costs) and **"Add for a Full Renovation"** (the incremental items to go from a softgoods refresh to a gut renovation) — covering Guestrooms, Guest Bathrooms, Corridors, Lobby, Public Restrooms, Restaurant, Bar/Lounge, Kitchen, Function Space, Exercise Facility, Pool, Parking, Landscaping, and a **Common Additives** section (PTAC units, elevator modernization, key systems, laundry equipment, ADA guestroom conversions, signage, entry doors, etc.) — confirming the Guide gives true **per-item unit costs by quality tier**, not just blended per-key averages (though it also provides per-key/per-SF summary rollups for quick estimating).

### A4. JLL / CBRE hotel capex publications

- **JLL** publishes a free (no signup encountered) *U.S. Select-Service and Extended-Stay Hotel Outlook* that touches on capex/development cost context but is a market outlook, not an itemized cost guide: https://www.esla.org/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2025/02/jll-us-select-service-and-extended-stay-hotel-outlook-2025.pdf — it does not give per-item unit costs (no PTAC $, no carpet $/sy, etc.); it is macro (development cost per room ranges only).
- I could not locate a JLL or CBRE publication with Nehmer/HVS-style itemized unit-cost tables (PTAC $, casegoods $/room, reglaze $, etc.) that is both public and post-2019. CBRE hotel research is generally behind their research-portal registration (lead-gen) — skipped per guardrails.
- **HVS's own U.S. Hotel Development Cost Survey** (ground-up construction, not renovation) is openly summarized in article form (no form encountered): https://www.hvs.com/article/10219-hvs-us-hotel-development-cost-survey-2025 and https://www.hvs.com/article/9999-HVS-US-Hotel-Development-Cost-Survey-2024 — useful as a *development* cost anchor (not renovation), reporting midscale extended-stay development at ~$167,000–$169,000 per room and upscale extended-stay at ~$265,000 per room (2024 data, per the 2025 survey). Flagged here as development, not renovation, cost — do not conflate the two in the model.

**Bottom line for Part A:** Yes, a real, form-free, publicly hosted copy of the Nehmer/HVS Hotel Cost Estimating Guide exists (2021 edition), and it is highly granular — true per-item unit costs (PTAC, carpet and pad, casegoods installation, bathroom regrout, bedsets, key systems, elevator modernization, etc.) broken out by six quality tiers, with both softgoods and full-renovation add-on layers. No comparably granular JLL/CBRE public renovation cost guide was found.

**Read-through for the model:** the two computable Park Hotels data points (~$254K/key Royal Palm, ~$276K/key Ali'i Tower) are both **major gut renovations of full-service, luxury/upper-upscale resort assets including public-area and amenity work**, not simple softgoods refreshes — treat them as a ceiling anchor for "comprehensive luxury renovation," not a typical mid-cycle PIP. They corroborate the Nehmer/HVS Guide's luxury tier "full renovation additional cost per key" figures (Guide 2021: $24,283–$32,954/key, pre-inflation) being directionally low for 2025–2026 full-scope luxury resort work once public space, amenities, and market-specific (Hawaii, Miami) cost premiums are included.

**Gap called out plainly:** I could not find any REIT-grade (audited, investor-deck-disclosed) per-key renovation figure for the **economy or economy-extended-stay** tier — this segment is dominated by privately held franchisors (G6/Blackstone) and franchise systems (Wyndham, Choice) that disclose *program existence* (Super 8 INNOV8TE, OYO's owner-capex model) but not detailed avg-cost-per-key figures the way REITs disclose $/key or $/room renovation spend in earnings decks.
