# Sourced Starter Benchmark Table — Hotel Renovation / PIP Costs

Compiled 2026-07-18 from free/public sources only. No purchased reports, no proprietary data. Every figure below has a live URL. Figures marked **[unclear]** or **[estimated]** are explicitly flagged as gaps, not fabricated benchmarks.

This seeds the demo dataset for an owner-side PIP bid-benchmarking tool. Precision of citation matters more than volume — where a source only gave a range without scope detail, that limitation is stated rather than papered over.

### Suggested application for the product (methodology note, not a published claim)

- **National baseline escalator, 2024→2026 dollars:** use the Turner YoY figure of +4.87% (Q1 2025→Q1 2026) as the general default, since it is the most recent, most granular, and most transparently methodological (labor + material + market competitiveness index) of the free indices found. Compound two years of RLB's "~4% annualized" as a cross-check — the two track closely (4.87% vs. ~4%), which is reassuring for using either as a default multiplier of roughly **1.04–1.05x per year**.
- **Regional adjustment:** where a specific market is known, prefer the RLB regional YoY figures (Honolulu +5.93%, Phoenix +5.30%, Miami +4.99%, DC +4.01%, Dallas +3.88%, Chicago +1.42%) over the national blend — variance between markets is large enough (1.4% to 5.9%) that a flat national number will materially misstate hot markets.
- **PIP-specific figures pre-date 2026** (the $35–40K/key Hunter Hotel Advisors figure and the Hampton/Holiday Inn Express figures from the HVS Industry Insights article are undated/2024-vintage). Apply the Turner or RLB general escalator above rather than assuming PIP costs escalate identically to new-construction costs — **[estimated methodology, no direct PIP-specific escalation index was found]**.

## (c) Honest coverage assessment

### Solid public anchors (usable as demo-dataset numbers with citation)
- **Overall per-key development cost by chain scale** (limited-service through luxury) — HVS 2025 Dev Cost Survey is a single authoritative, named, dated, free source with clean segment breakdowns. Good backbone number, though it's *development* not *renovation* cost.
- **Specific brand-program PIP costs for two major midscale/upper-midscale brands** — Hampton "Forever Young" and Holiday Inn Express "Formula Blue" have unusually granular, multi-tier (by building vintage) figures from the HVS Industry Insights article, including sub-line-items (exterior/roofline, guestroom-only, public-space). This is the best category-level data found and should anchor the midscale/upper-midscale tier of the demo dataset.
- **Midmarket PIP per-key blended figure** ($35–40K/key) — attributed by name to a specific person/firm (Lee Hunter, Hunter Hotel Advisors) via the Matthews article, which is a reasonably credible secondary citation of a primary broker view.
- **National construction cost escalation** — Turner and RLB indices are both live, quarterly-updated, named, dated, freely accessible, and converge closely (~4-5%/year), giving genuine confidence in a national escalator.
- **Regional cost escalation variance** — RLB's city-level breakdown is solid and recent (Q2 2026).
- **FF&E tariff rates** — the Section 232/301/IEEPA rate structure is reasonably well documented for 2025–2026, though dollar-cost pass-through to hoteliers (vs. tariff percentage) is not quantified anywhere found.

### Gaps requiring labeled interpolation
- **Luxury and upper-upscale PIP costs specifically (not just development cost)**: no primary broker/HVS source was found quoting PIP-specific per-key figures for luxury/upper-upscale brands (Marriott Autograph/Luxury Collection, Hilton Waldorf/LXR, IHG InterContinental tiers, etc.). All luxury renovation figures found came from SEO aggregator sites (Sara Hospitality USA, George Stones/George Solution network) with inconsistent, non-attributable ranges ($80K–$205K/room vs. $33K–$46K/room vs. >$120K/room across different pages from what appears to be the same content network). **These must be labeled [estimated] in the demo dataset, not presented as sourced.**
- **Extended-stay and economy-tier PIP-specific costs** (as opposed to development cost): similarly thin. Economy figures ($4K–$20K/room range) come mostly from aggregator content, not brokerage/HVS primary sources.
- **MEP/infrastructure and elevator costs specific to hotels**: only generic CRE elevator-modernization figures were found (not hotel PIP-specific), plus one low-credibility contractor-estimator page for hotel elevators. No brand PIP source was found itemizing MEP (electrical, plumbing, HVAC, life-safety/sprinkler, elevator) as a distinct PIP line item with a dollar figure.
- **Corridor-specific and lobby-specific per-key costs**: found only as part of Holiday Inn Express's "public-space reconfiguration" lump sum ($200K–$400K total) — not disaggregated into corridor vs. lobby vs. F&B outlet.
- **The full 2025/2026 Nehmer & HVS Design Hotel Cost Estimating Guide** — this is very likely the single best primary source for category × tier cost data (guestrooms, bathrooms, corridors, public space, F&B outlets, function space, amenities, infrastructure, common additives, all across Economy through Luxury), but the actual line-item figures are locked behind a guide download (hotelcostestimatingguide.com / nehmer.com/costguide) that requires registration. **News coverage of its release confirms the guide's structure and category taxonomy but not its numbers.** This is the single highest-value gap: if the tool can legitimately access the guide (free registration, not a paid purchase) in a later phase, it should replace most of the [estimated] cells above.
- **JLL and CBRE renovation-capex-specific figures**: neither firm's free public content yielded a specific renovation-capex-per-key number for 2025/2026; both were general market-outlook narratives (JLL Global Hotel Investment Outlook 2026, CBRE Hotel Horizons) confirming renovation activity is "muted" and capex needs are "stronger" qualitatively, with no quantified figure free of a paywall/subscription.
- **Direct FF&E tariff dollar pass-through**: percentages for tariff rates are documented, but no source converts that into a "$X more per key" or "$X more per case-good order" figure — this conversion, if used in the product, must be clearly labeled as the tool's own estimated calculation, not a cited figure.

### Recommendation for demo dataset construction
Use HVS Dev Cost Survey (A1) + HVS Industry Insights PIP brand data (A2, Hampton/Holiday Inn Express rows) + Hunter Hotel Advisors $35–40K/key (A2) as the sourced backbone for midscale/upper-midscale. Mark every luxury, extended-stay, MEP, elevator, and corridor/lobby-specific cell as **[estimated]** with a note that it's interpolated from adjacent categories/tiers, pending either (1) free registration access to the Nehmer/HVS Cost Estimating Guide, or (2) additional primary-source hunting in a later phase.
