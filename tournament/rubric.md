# Tournament Rubric — Run 1

Designed before Phase 1 results were in, so the scoring frame isn't shaped to fit a favorite. Weights reflect the mission: a company Andrew could take to market this month, largely solo, with no outside spending.

## Scoring dimensions (0–10 each)

| Dimension | Weight | What a 10 looks like |
|---|---|---|
| **Pain** | 20% | Documented, recent, first-person anger; large dollar stakes per incident |
| **Urgency** | 15% | The buyer is in an active bleeding moment with a deadline (a disputed invoice, a mandated PIP), not a "nice someday" improvement |
| **Reachability** | 15% | A definable buyer you can find and contact cheaply (named associations, conferences, LinkedIn titles, franchise directories) |
| **Willingness to pay** | 20% | Evidence buyers already pay adjacent humans (consultants, lawyers, auditors) real money for this exact outcome |
| **Buildability** | 15% | A solo founder + AI can ship a credible v1 this month with free/available data; no cold-start network effect; no licensing wall |
| **Incumbent weakness** | 15% | Incumbents exist (proves market) but are mispositioned, overpriced, or serve a different side of the table |

Weighted score = Σ(dimension × weight). Pain and WTP weigh heaviest because the reference run's core lesson (and Andrew's stated priority) is stress-testing viability *before* building.

## Structure

1. **Round 1 — Panel scoring.** Five judge personas independently score every entrant (4 seeded candidates, any scout-surfaced candidates, and 2–3 designed combinations). Each judge returns dimension scores + a one-paragraph rationale, grounded ONLY in the Phase 1 verified evidence files.
2. **Round 2 — Advocate/skeptic duels.** Top 3 by weighted average each get one advocate brief (strongest honest case) and one skeptic brief (strongest honest kill case), both citing evidence files.
3. **Round 3 — Fresh judge vote.** Three fresh judges read the duel briefs and vote for a winner with reasoning.
4. **Final call — Fable.** I make the final decision, allowed to overrule the vote only with written reasoning (e.g., if judges missed a guardrail conflict like an unlicensable data source).

## Judge personas (Round 1)

1. **The Burned Owner** — independent hotel owner, 120-key franchise property, has personally eaten a surprise renovation overrun. Scores from "would I actually pull out a credit card."
2. **The Asset Manager** — institutional hotel asset manager overseeing 15 properties' capex. Scores from portfolio-level utility and procurement reality (can this get through a corporate vendor process, or is it a credit-card SaaS?).
3. **The Bootstrapped SaaS Operator** — has built and sold a niche vertical-SaaS. Scores from CAC/LTV instinct, channel reality, and whether a solo founder can support it.
4. **The Construction Attorney** — hospitality construction lawyer. Scores from legal exposure (defamation, unauthorized practice of law, reliance risk) and from where legal spend proves willingness to pay.
5. **The Data Skeptic** — ex-data-broker product lead. Scores purely on whether the data the product depends on is actually obtainable, fresh, and defensible at v1 scale.

## Combination entrants (to be finalized after Phase 1)

Candidates 1, 2, and 4 share one buyer (owner/asset manager making a construction decision) at three moments: before renovation (2: is this PIP bid fair), at GC selection (4: does this GC have a pattern), during construction (1: is this change order fair). A combined "owner's construction defense" product is a mandatory entrant. Candidate 3 joins a combination only if Phase 1 overturns its weak WTP signal.
