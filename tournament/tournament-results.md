## Final ruling (Fable, top-level)

**Winner: E2 — PIP Bid Benchmark.** The panel ranked it first, both cast votes crowned it, and its kill case (competitor stall = no demand) is an interpretation, while every alternative carries an unrebutted structural defect (self-disowned premise, paywalled core data, unreachable second buyer).

**Conditions adopted as binding design constraints** (merged from both votes):
1. **v1 scope locked**: PIP benchmarking calculator + negotiation/pushback memo generator. No reserve-forecasting bundle, no GC dossier. E5/E6 material goes to the roadmap section of the business plan only.
2. **Claims discipline**: market with 2025–2026-dated evidence only (CMBS wave, tariff inflation, current trade coverage). Never use HREC 2016 / Magnuson 2016 anecdotes as current pricing; never repeat the unverified "10yr→7yr PIP cycle" claim.
3. **Differentiation must be explicit** vs PIPGURU/PIPGENIUS: the negotiation memo output, turnaround speed, brand/line-item depth, and evidence-cited benchmarks — price alone cannot be the wedge.
4. **Demand-validation plan documented, not executed**: judges asked for pre-sales tests / 10–15 owner conversations. The publish-nothing guardrail prohibits outreach during this run, so this becomes step one of the launch plan handed to Andrew, stated honestly as the biggest open risk.
5. **AAHOA treated as an audience, not an endorsing partner** (PIPs are not on its formal 12 Points agenda).
6. **Liability posture**: product language stays benchmarking/negotiation-support — no legal opinions, no engineering opinions.
7. **Monitor tripwire**: if PIPGURU/PIPGENIUS launches with real customers, the "undefended niche" premise collapses — flagged in the business plan's risk register.
