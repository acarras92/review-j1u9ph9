# PIPBench — Voiceover Scripts

Both voiceovers are generated with a Microsoft Edge neural TTS voice (en-US-AndrewNeural) — a free, no-signup local pipeline. No voice cloning was used because no voice assets exist in this project; the founder video is presented honestly as an AI-voiced founder note, not a simulated human on camera.

## Launch video VO (~60s as generated; the rendered video runs 63s with scene padding)

Note (stranger-test fix): the VO line "the nine standard renovation categories" refers to the Nehmer/HVS industry taxonomy the product targets; the demo built this run implements the subset exercised by its sample scope (see `business/business-plan.md` §4.1).

A PIP just landed on your desk. Six figures, maybe seven — and a deadline. The old way to check that number: a five-to-fifteen-thousand-dollar consultant, and a three-week wait.

PIPBench is the new way. Paste the scope. PIPBench parses every line, sorts it into the nine standard renovation categories, and benchmarks each one against named public cost sources — the industry cost guide, brand program disclosures, franchise filings — escalated to twenty-twenty-six dollars, and graded honestly: verified, or directional.

Then it writes the memo. What to accept. What to question. What to counter — with the evidence cited on every line.

In this demo, a five point seven million dollar bid gave up two hundred and sixty-six thousand dollars in above-range pricing — plus a documented alternative scope worth up to one point one million more.

Your PIP isn't a bill. It's an opening offer. PIPBench — the owner-side PIP benchmark.

## Founder video VO (~30s)

So I built PIPBench. It benchmarks every line of your PIP against public cost sources — sources you can open and check yourself — and writes the memo you take back to the brand.
