Company Builder — Run 1 Recap

One autonomous run. One company: PIPBench — benchmark your hotel's brand-mandated renovation plan, then take the memo to the negotiation.

A PIP — Property Improvement Plan — is the renovation scope a hotel brand (Marriott, Hilton, IHG…) mandates a franchise owner complete, often six or seven figures, issued on brand cycles and at every sale. PIPBench benchmarks that demand against named public cost sources and writes the owner's negotiation memo. Built from the open internet in a single unattended session on 2026-07-18: research, tournament selection, brand, working product, landing page, videos, business plan, and a 38-attack red team — every claim source-cited, every guardrail kept. What happened — nine phases, unattended

1

Pain hunt & verification

9 research agents re-verified all four seeded candidates claim-by-claim (26 claims: verified/changed/unverifiable), swept Reddit, trade press, and forums for 33 organic complaints, and scouted new candidates. A completeness critic failed the phase and forced 4 targeted gap-fills.

2

Tournament

5 judge personas scored 8 entrants (4 seeded + 2 scouted + 2 designed combinations) on a pre-registered rubric; advocates and skeptics dueled over the finalists; fresh judges voted. PIP bid benchmarking won — the only candidate with hotel-specific, 2026-dated pain evidence and no structural defect.

3

Design research

Data-source feasibility (the make-or-break question), competitor teardowns, ICP/market math, pricing architecture. Key finds: the 2021 Nehmer/HVS cost guide openly hosted with true per-item unit costs; FDD conversion tables as free public anchors; both "competitors" are pre-revenue sites; the analyst-service competitor explicitly refuses to support negotiation — the exact wedge PIPBench takes.

4

Brand

PIPBench — pipbench.com verified available, no collisions found. Logo: a surveyor's benchmark (a verified public reference point), chosen from four rendered candidates. Tagline: "Your PIP isn't a bill. It's an opening offer."

5

Build

An evidence-graded benchmark dataset (every figure source-cited, VERIFIED/DIRECTIONAL badges), a working 4-step demo (parse → benchmark → memo), the landing page, business plan, and launch plan.

6

Media

Screen-recorded demo GIF, a 63-second launch video, and a 31-second founder note — all produced locally with free tooling and honest labeling (no avatar/voice assets existed in this project, so nothing human was simulated).

7

Red team

Six skeptics filed 38 attacks; every one was ruled on: 12 fixed in the artifacts this run, 18 bound into the plans, 8 accepted as documented risks, 0 rejected, 0 kills. Two severity-5 findings reshaped the plan: willingness-to-pay is untested (validation is now a hard launch gate) and free AI chatbots are a permanent substitution ceiling (answered with the sourced-and-graded data strategy).

What we know vs. what we don't (the honesty register)

The strongest thing this run can hand you is a clear map of which load-bearing facts are proven and which are still hypotheses.

KNOWN  The pain is real and 2026-dated. PIP costs, tariffs, cost escalation, the CMBS maturity wave, and owner distress are all primary-sourced (see the site's "2026 squeeze" section — every stat links to its source).

KNOWN  The wedge is unoccupied. No competitor ships a negotiation memo; the analyst-model competitor explicitly excludes negotiation support; the data can be legitimately assembled from public sources with honest grading.

UNKNOWN  Willingness to pay. No owner has said "I'd pay $399 for this." The validation protocol (smoke test + 10–15 owner calls + 5 broker calls, thresholds pre-registered) is a hard gate before any real spend.

UNKNOWN  Market size. ~5,000–9,000 PIPs/year is a derived, labeled estimate — the derivation is shown in the business plan, and revenue scenarios carry the honest floor (~$14K/yr at worst-case assumptions).

RISK  Free AI substitution and platform entry. A general chatbot can produce a rough version of this for free; HVS/Nehmer own the best data; PIPGURU now has a live signup page. The counters: source-cited trust, a licensing/partnership approach to the data publisher, and an outcome-data flywheel — all bound into the plans by the red team.
